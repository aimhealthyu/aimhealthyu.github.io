What Organizational Apathy Reveals About Security Culture in the Tech Industry (2025 Insight)

▍ The quiet “whatever” that kills your security The weird thing I keep noticing lately isn’t zero-days, it’s shrugs. Not “we don’t have budget”. It’s “yeah I know this is bad, but… whatever, ship it”. That “whatever” is basically your real security posture. I remember reading an internal post-mortem from a big SaaS shop — the root cause wasn’t missing MFA or some fancy exploit, it was: someone filed a risk ticket, someone else commented “good catch”, then nobody touched it for 9 months while the feature scaled to millions of users. That’s organizational apathy in 2025: 【everyone intellectually agrees security matters，but nobody feels it enough to move their calendar】. And you can see culture through the tiny frictions: ・ Oncall runbooks updated instantly? or “I’ll fix the doc after this incident”… for 8 incidents straight ・ Slack #security channel = real questions? or just compliance announcements nobody reads ・ PM 在 Jira 裡看到 “High risk” tag，第一反應是「要談 trade-off」，還是「先問清楚影響再說」 When I was chatting with一個在新加坡雲端廠工作的朋友，他說他們有所有 fancy controls：SSO, Okta, CrowdStrike, the whole zoo。 但 phishing drill 點開率還是卡在大概 20% 多一點——因為員工知道「最慘也就是被叫去再上一次 training」，沒有真實後果。 So the apathy is a metric, honestly. Not “number of vulns”, but things like： ・ Time from “we know this is bad” → “someone actually owns it” ・ How often security is the reason to say NO… vs the reason to redesign something smarter ・ Whether engineers feel safer shipping fast or safer raising a red flag If those numbers are ugly, your culture slide deck is lying.
